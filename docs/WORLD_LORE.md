# The Cascade — World Primer

*115 years since the world began counting again.*

## The Shape of the World

Long before recorded time, a floating city called **Eris** stood above the
world, wielding technology indistinguishable from magic — matter
manipulation, instantaneous travel, forces no modern scholar can fully
explain. Then Eris fell. No one agrees on how: a war, an accident, a
supernova of its own making. What's certain is that it scattered — hull
fragments, dead machines, humming cores — across the continent, and the
world had to rebuild without it.

That rebuilding is why **The Cascade** looks the way it does: a
**semi-futuristic** world where a handful of mega-cities run on
Eris-descended technology — Void-matter reactors, autonomous mechs,
holographic engineering, short-range teleportation called **Voidwarp** —
while everywhere just outside their light, people still burn wood,
scavenge, and fix things by hand. Glacier 15's few survivors didn't
rebuild with reactors; they broke into abandoned train tunnels with metal
pipes and stole heat-devices to survive the winter. That gap — a starship
next to a campfire — is the world's real texture, and it's deliberate: the
"advanced" places are advanced because they hoarded what fell from Eris,
and the primitive places are primitive because they didn't get any.

## Ninety Years of Uneasy Peace, and Where It Broke

Ninety years ago the surviving powers met at the first Icon Summit and
agreed to a fragile, mutual peace over Eris's remains and the world it
left behind. Three nations emerged from that treaty as its stewards:
**Acatrya**, led by **Xender**; the **H-Nation**, led by **HHyper**; and a
third power that history has mostly forgotten. For decades it mostly held.

Then **Ocellios Labs** happened. Founded by the reclusive inventor
**Stubby**, Ocellios became the source of nearly all modern
infrastructure — and, eventually, of Void-matter synthesis, the energy
source every city now depends on. Xender secured exclusive rights to it,
freezing out the H-Nation and souring the peace. Rumors followed the lab
for years: unauthorized experiments, disappearances, research that pushed
too close to what killed Eris in the first place. Nobody could prove it.

Then **Glacier 15** — once a thriving city — was reduced to frozen
rubble in a single night, officially an "unknown incident," unofficially
covered up so thoroughly that survivors like Josh couldn't get anyone to
admit people had gone missing at all. It was the first thread anyone
managed to pull. Investigative journalists at **The Daily Dolphe**,
Cascade's founding news outlet, kept pulling it until the outlet itself
was shut down for it — and reformed instead as **Team Cascade**, an
independent resistance dedicated to dragging the truth out from under
Xender's regime by whatever means necessary: reporting, smuggling,
sabotage, and eventually open war.

## Team Cascade

This is the faction the player belongs to. Not a government, not an
army — a decentralized network of mercenaries, engineers, ex-journalists,
and defectors held together by Dolphe, the paper's original founder, now
running a resistance instead of a press. Cascade's edge isn't firepower;
it's that they go where the "advanced" nations won't — into Eris wreckage,
Void-poisoned wastelands, and dead cities like Glacier 15 that Acatrya and
the H-Nation have written off — and they bring back what they find. Every
dungeon expedition in this game is, narratively, a Cascade operation: a
supply run, a relic recovery, a chance to recover one more fragment of
what actually happened at Ocellios and Glacier 15.

## Regions

- **Glacier 15** — a permafrost ruin, once a city, now a frozen expedition
  site laced with collapsed train tunnels and rogue security drones that
  never got the shutdown order.
- **The Wastelands** — irradiated-adjacent scrubland outside Acatrya's
  cities, home to strikers, protestors, and the people the "advanced"
  world left behind.
- **The Hotlands** — volcanic, resource-rich, and fought over; the
  Xendium supercomputer labs are buried here.
- **The Voidcrest Desert / Void Crevasse** — the site of the original
  Void-matter discovery, now heavily militarized and half-consumed by
  unstable rifts.
- **Abyssnia** — the glittering capital of Acatrya, all towers and
  advertisements, where a fifth of the population sleeps in the alleys
  behind them.

## Threads a Cascade Operative Might Uncover

Story-room fragments found mid-expedition should feel like discovering
pieces of this — a torn Ocellios research memo, a Glacier 15 evacuation
order that was never sent, a maintenance log for a Voidwarp rift that
shouldn't exist, a name (Rex, Subject 29, Flux) that means nothing to the
player yet and everything to someone who survived it.
